    Photo Tips for Better Diagnosis
    🔍
    Get as close as possible
    Fill the frame with a single leaf showing clear symptoms. Blurry or distant photos dramatically reduce accuracy.
    ☀️
    Use natural daylight
    Natural light shows true colours — critical for disease identification. Avoid harsh noon sun; morning or overcast light is ideal.
    🌿
    Show both sides of the leaf
    Many diseases — like rusts, downy mildew, and bacterial pustules — show their most diagnostic symptoms on the underside. Take two photos.
    📍
    Choose a representative leaf
    Pick a leaf that shows typical symptoms — not the most extreme or the least affected. Early lesions (not yet merged) give the best diagnosis clues.
    📱
    Tap to focus before capturing
    Tap on the disease lesion on your phone screen to lock focus there before taking the photo.
    ❌
    Avoid these mistakes
    Don't photograph a wet leaf (water droplets obscure symptoms). Don't pick the leaf off the plant first. Don't use a dark background. Don't photograph in shade so dark that colours are distorted.
